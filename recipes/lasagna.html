Lasagna

Description

Lasagna, a beloved Italian dish that has become a global comfort food favorite, consists of layers of wide pasta sheets alternating with rich sauces, cheese, and various fillings. This hearty dish originated in the Emilia-Romagna region of Italy, particularly in the city of Bologna, where it was traditionally made with ragù (meat sauce), béchamel sauce, and Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese. The pasta sheets themselves, when made fresh, are crafted from flour, eggs, and sometimes a pinch of salt, creating a tender yet sturdy foundation for the multiple layers. The beauty of lasagna lies in its versatility and the way its flavors meld together during baking. As the dish bakes, the pasta layers absorb the sauces while the cheese melts and creates a golden-brown crust on top. While traditional Italian lasagna follows specific regional recipes, modern variations have emerged worldwide, incorporating different ingredients like spinach, mushrooms, seafood, or even making vegetarian versions with roasted vegetables and alternative cheese combinations. The dish typically needs to rest for about 15 minutes after baking, allowing it to set properly and making it easier to cut into neat portions.

Ingredients

For the Meat Sauce:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 pound Italian sausage
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cans (28 oz each) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Béchamel Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt and white pepper to taste

Additional Components:

  • 1 package lasagna noodles
  • 16 oz mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese, grated
  • 15 oz ricotta cheese
  • 2 eggs (for ricotta mixture)
  • Fresh basil leaves

Preparation Steps:

  1. Prepare the Meat Sauce:

    1. Brown meat in a large pot
    2. Add onions and garlic, cook until softened
    3. Add tomatoes, paste, and seasonings
    4. Simmer for 1 hour
    5. Adjust seasonings to taste

  2. Make the Béchamel:

    1. Melt butter in a saucepan
    2. Add flour and cook for 2-3 minutes
    3. Gradually whisk in warm milk
    4. Cook until thickened
    5. Season with nutmeg, salt, and pepper

  3. Prepare Ricotta Mixture:

    1. Mix ricotta with eggs
    2. Add 1/2 cup Parmigiano-Reggiano
    3. Season with salt and pepper

  4. Cook Pasta:

    1. Boil lasagna noodles until al dente
    2. Drain and lay flat to prevent sticking

  5. Assemble Lasagna:

    1. Spread meat sauce on bottom of baking dish
    2. Layer: noodles, meat sauce, ricotta mixture, béchamel, mozzarella
    3. Repeat layers 3-4 times
    4. Top with remaining cheese

  6. Bake:

    1. Cover with foil
    2. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25 minutes
    3. Remove foil and bake 25 more minutes until golden
    4. Let rest 15 minutes before serving

Tips:

  • Make sauces a day ahead for better flavor
  • No-boil noodles can be used to save time
  • Cover with foil if top browns too quickly
  • Freeze unbaked lasagna for up to 3 months
